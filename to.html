Chapter 1: The Discovery

Max Forrester had always considered himself an unremarkable man. At thirty-two, he lived alone in a modest apartment, worked as a history professor at the local community college, and spent most of his free time buried in books about ancient civilizations. His colleagues often joked that he knew more about people who had been dead for centuries than those currently alive. They weren't entirely wrong.

It was on a rainy Tuesday afternoon when Max's life changed forever. He had been browsing through a small antique shop that had recently opened on the corner of his street. The shop was cluttered with dusty artifacts, forgotten treasures, and items whose origins were lost to time. The air smelled of old paper and polished wood.

"Can I help you find anything specific?" asked the shopkeeper, an elderly woman with silver hair and eyes that seemed to hold secrets of their own.

"Just looking," Max replied, his attention already captured by something in the corner of the room.

It was a mirror, unlike any he had seen before. The frame was made of dark wood, intricately carved with symbols he didn't recognize—and as a historian, that was unusual. The glass itself seemed to shimmer slightly, even in the dim light of the shop. Max approached it cautiously, drawn by an inexplicable pull.

"Ah, you have good taste," the shopkeeper said, appearing beside him. "That piece is quite special. They say it once belonged to an alchemist who claimed it showed more than just reflections."

Max smiled politely at what he assumed was a sales pitch. "It's beautiful craftsmanship. How much?"

The price was surprisingly reasonable, almost suspiciously so for such an elaborate piece. Before he could reconsider, Max found himself carrying the mirror back to his apartment, struggling against the rain and wind as if nature itself was trying to prevent him from taking it home.

That night, as thunder crashed outside his window, Max hung the mirror on the wall of his study. For a moment, he stood before it, examining his reflection. Nothing seemed unusual—just the same tired eyes, the same disheveled brown hair, the same unremarkable Max Forrester.

He turned away to prepare for bed, unaware that behind him, the surface of the mirror rippled like water disturbed by a gentle breeze. By morning, his world would never be the same again.
